I am a junior undergraduate at UCLA in the department of mathematics, broadly interested in algebraic geometry and its applications.

[Here's my professional life in Math.](/Bockman_Cheung_CV__Mar_2026.pdf)

The purpose of my website, other than letting mathematicians find me, is selfish: I want to explore my opinions, my prejudices, and come to know myself better from reflections.

In math, I grapple with the question of what I should take interest in, for any of its branches seems infinite in possibility in the individual scale. To summarize abstractly, I try to find my voice that defines me as a mathematician and from it, derive my happiness.

Coming to early adulthood, I realize life also is about finding my own voice and deciding what makes me happy. 

I strive to be a human in this grand uphill of life. To experience the joy of bonding with others. To exercise my values. To pursue knowledge and wisdom. To be in communion with nature.

This blog is thus about what I find colorful, unapologetic, and meaningful that defends, demonstrates, and glorifies my humanity.
